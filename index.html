In July 1916, five people were "attacked" by sharks while swimming along the coast of New Jersey. Since then, the media and popular culture have unjustly characterized sharks as predators with an insatiable appetite for human flesh.
But, nothing could be further from the truth.
What if we were to redefine the relationship we have with sharks to one based on scientific data rather than one based on limited an biased information?
In the visual story that unfolds, we will show you how changing the way we perceive sharks could change our environment, our economies, and our lives for the better.

What can you do to help?
  • Donate money to shark conservation causes. A few worthy causes include:
    • Atlantic White Shark Conservancy
    • Shark Research Institute
    • Florida Museum of Natural History
  • Do not eat shark fin soup (and discourage businesses from selling it).
  • Do not consume shark meat.
  • Do not purchase souvenirs or pharmaceuticals made from shark body parts.
  • Further educate yourself and raise awareness about the plight of sharks.
  • Oh, and don't refer to encounters between sharks and humans as "attacks" (by now, you should know that they don't "attack" us).
