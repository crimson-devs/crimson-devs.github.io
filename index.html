In July 1916, Sharks attacked and bit 5 people at New Jersey Shore beaches in the United States.
Since then, the media and popular culture have portrayed sharks as mythical "predators" with an insatiable appetite for

human flesh.

This could NOT be further from the TRUTH.

"There is a creature alive today, which has survived millions of years of evolution... without change, without passion, without logic... It lives to kill. A mindless eating machine, it will attack and devour anything." Jaws (1975)
The earliest account of a Shark Attack fatality was unearthed, figuratively and literally, in Japan, by scientists at Oxford last year.
We will combine data and science to separate insights from noise.
Part art and part science will allow us to learn the nature of the beast across space and time.
There are shark species, divided between 8 orders.
Only 20 species have ever bitten a human.
3 species account for more than 50% of the bites and 90% of the human fatalities.

What can WE do to help?
What can you do to help?
  • Donate money to shark conservation causes and continued research. A few worthy causes include:
    • Atlantic White Shark Conservancy
    • Shark Research Institute
    • Florida Museum of Natural History
  • Do not consume shark products
    • Shark Fin Soup
    • Shark Meat
    • Pharmaceuticals
    • Souvenirs
  • Further educate yourself and raise awareness about the plight of sharks.
  • Refrain from referring to shark bites as "attacks". Doing so only perpetuates the myth that sharks are man-eating predators!
